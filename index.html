How It Works

01
📚
Choose Your Grade
Open your grade's journal. Fill in your name and school on the cover. Each journal is built for exactly where you are in your reading life.
02
📖
Read the Books
Work through the reading list — required first, then your choice, then your independent selection. Mark your status as you go. Your progress is saved automatically.
03
✍️
Journal as You Read
Answer the SAT-aligned prompts, cite the text, build your vocabulary list with parts of speech and definitions, and try the practice question for each book.
04
🖨️
Print or Export
When you're done, use the Print button to save as a PDF or print your journal to turn in. Or export all your responses as a text file. Your teacher gets everything.
04

SAT Skills Built In

Every prompt in every journal targets a specific, named SAT skill. Here's what you're building all summer.

🎯
Claim · Evidence · Explanation
The foundational SAT writing pattern. Every journal prompt requires you to make a claim, quote the text, and explain the connection. CEE is practiced in every book, every grade.
All Grades
📖
Vocabulary in Context
Each journal has a vocabulary builder where you record words with their part of speech, context definition, and an original sentence — exactly how the SAT tests vocabulary.
All Grades
🧭
Point of View & Narration
From Scout's child perspective to Offred's reconstructed testimony, journals ask how narrator choice shapes meaning — a top-frequency SAT Reading question type.
9th10th11th
⚖️
Argument Analysis
Identify an author's central claim, evaluate their evidence, assess reasoning quality, and address counterarguments. Practiced with Fitzgerald, Miller, Dostoevsky, and Atwood.
10th11th12th
🔗
Paired Text Synthesis
Every independent reading prompt asks you to compare two books: shared theme, key difference, and what reading them together reveals. This is the SAT's hardest reading skill.
10th11th12th
🖊️
Analytical Essay Writing
9th grade writes paragraphs. 11th writes 3-paragraph essays. 12th writes full 4-paragraph analytical essays with thesis, development, and significance — the complete SAT essay arc.
